## Slide 3 — The Problem (Builder's Pain)

Three problems every builder in Telangana faces:

1. **Slow walls** — Standard bricks + mortar = skilled labor + time. Labour is scarce and expensive.
2. **Inconsistent supply** — Fly ash depends on power plant schedules. Your project stalls when supply stalls.
3. **No eco option** — Green building projects (IGBC, PMAY eco) cannot source certified bricks locally.

**Quote from the data:** *"Only 13.2% of 355 factories have a website. The entire industry is offline, informal, and fragmented."*

## Appendix A — Why Interlocking Beats Standard Bricks

**For a 1,000 sq ft house (ground-floor walls)** — ~18,000 standard bricks **or ~8,500 CSEB blocks** (one block does the wall work of ~2 bricks).

| | Standard brick | Mud interlocking (CSEB) |
|---|---|---|
| Unit cost | ₹1.26 lakh (18,000 × ₹7) | ₹2.38 lakh (8,500 × ₹28) |
| Mortar cost | ₹45,000 | ₹12,000 (internal walls dry-stack) |
| Labour (laying) | ₹60,000 | ₹40,000 (faster, less skilled) |
| Plastering (interior) | ₹30,000 | ₹0 (optional natural finish) |
| **Total wall cost** | **₹2.61 lakh** | **₹2.90 lakh** |
| Speed | 25 days | 16 days |
| Carbon footprint | Medium | Low (no kiln) |
| IGBC green points | No | Yes |

*Honest read: CSEB carries a modest material premium. After lower mortar, labour and plastering, the total wall lands within ~10% of conventional cost — while building 9 days faster, with a natural finish and IGBC eligibility. **This is a premium green product, not the cheapest brick.** For green-rated, speed-sensitive, or quality-led projects, that premium is easily justified — and at bulk pricing (₹26/block) the gap narrows further.*

**Sell the speed, the finish, and the green rating — not just the price.**

## Appendix B — Soil Suitability (Telangana)

- **Ranga Reddy:** Red laterite soil — ideal for CSEB, well-studied, moderate clay content
- **Kamareddy:** Mixed red-black soil — good with 6% cement stabilization
- **Test required:** Atterberg limits, particle size analysis, linear shrinkage — cost ₹15,000 per site
- **Rule of thumb:** Avoid soils with >30% clay or organic matter. Telangana red soil is typically 15–22% clay — in the ideal range.
