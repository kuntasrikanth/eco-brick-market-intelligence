# Business Plan — Mud Interlocking Brick Factory
### Telangana, India | 2026

---

## 1. Executive Summary

**Company:** TerraBlock (working name)
**Product:** Fully Machined Compressed Mud Interlocking Bricks (CSEB)
**Location:** Ranga Reddy District, Telangana (Phase 1)
**Model:** B2B manufacturing — sell direct to contractors, builders, and housing projects
**Funding ask:** ₹75 lakh (seed / MSME loan)
**Break-even:** Month 16

### The Opportunity in One Paragraph
Telangana has 355 brick manufacturers. Of those, only 8 make interlocking blocks — all fly ash, none mud. Zero factories in the state produce Compressed Stabilized Earth Blocks (CSEB) at machine scale. The dominant product (hand-made fly ash brick) is cost-sensitive, commodity, and losing margin. Mud interlocking bricks cost 40% less to produce, require no kiln, no fly ash procurement, use locally available soil, and are gaining adoption in green building — yet the market has no organized supplier. This is a product gap, not a demand gap.

---

## 2. The Problem

### Builder Pain Points
- **Standard bricks need skilled labor** to lay — mortar, alignment, time. Interlocking bricks stack without mortar, reducing labor cost by 25–35%.
- **Fly ash supply is inconsistent** — sourced from power plants; price fluctuates with plant utilization.
- **No eco-certified product** available at scale in Telangana. Green building projects (IGBC/GRIHA rated) cannot source locally.
- **Construction timelines slip** — conventional brick walls are slow. Interlocking walls are 2–3x faster.

### Market Reality (from data)
- 55% of 355 factories are still hand-made — competitors are not modernizing.
- Only 13.2% of factories have a website — the industry is informal and fragmented.
- 101 factories (28.5%) are at high closure risk — supply consolidation is coming.
- Ranga Reddy: 1.34 million households, only 6 brick factories — the largest under-supplied district in Telangana.

## 3. The Product

### Compressed Stabilized Earth Block (CSEB) — Mud Interlocking Brick

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Material | Local red/black soil + 5–8% cement stabilizer |
| Production | Hydraulic press machine — no kiln, no firing |
| Size | 295 × 195 × 100 mm (standard interlocking profile) |
| Compressive strength | 3.5–7 N/mm² (meets IS 1725 / BIS standard) |
| Water absorption | < 20% |
| Feature | Self-interlocking edges — no mortar required for internal walls |
| Finish | Natural earth tone — no plastering needed (optional) |
| Certifications to target | BIS IS 1725, IGBC Green Materials, Telangana RERA compliance |

### Why Mud Over Fly Ash
| | Fly Ash Brick | Mud Interlocking Block (CSEB) |
|---|---|---|
| Unit | Standard brick | Block, 300×150×90 mm (≈ 2 bricks of wall) |
| Raw material | Fly ash (power plant sourced) | Local soil (free/cheap) |
| Energy | Kiln firing or steam curing | Zero — cold press |
| Production cost | ₹3.5–5.0/brick | ₹13–15/block |
| Selling price | ₹6–8/brick | ₹28/block (market ₹30–50) |
| Gross margin | 25–40% | 46–50% |
| Carbon footprint | Medium | Low (no kiln) |
| Differentiation | Commodity | Unique in Telangana market |

> CSEB is priced per block, not per brick. One block does the wall work of ~2 conventional bricks while needing little mortar — so comparing headline prices understates the value. Sell on total wall cost.

## 4. Market Analysis

### TAM — Total Addressable Market
- Telangana housing construction: ~3.5 lakh housing units/year (urban + rural)
- Average bricks per unit: 8,000–12,000
- Total brick demand: ~3.5 billion bricks/year
- At ₹8 average price: **TAM = ₹2,800 Cr/year**

### SAM — Serviceable Addressable Market
- Ranga Reddy + adjacent districts (Hyderabad, Medchal, Sangareddy, Kamareddy)
- ~30% of state construction activity
- **SAM = ₹840 Cr/year**

### SOM — Serviceable Obtainable Market (Year 3 target)
- Capture ~0.35% of SAM = ₹2.93 Cr revenue (Year 3)
- Equivalent to ~80,000 blocks/month at ₹30/block
- Achievable with 2 plants (4 machines) at ~65% utilization

### Target Customer Segments
1. **Tier 2 residential builders** (50–200 unit projects) — price-sensitive, open to cost savings on labor
2. **Affordable housing projects** (PMAY, TIDCO) — eco-mandate, bulk orders
3. **Commercial construction** (warehouses, factories) — large blocks, speed of construction
4. **Eco architects & green buildings** — IGBC rating requirements

## 6. Business Model

### Revenue Streams
1. **Block sales (primary)** — B2B bulk orders, ₹28–35/block
2. **Project supply contracts** — 6–12 month agreements with builders
3. **Premium certified product** — IGBC-rated blocks at ~25% premium for green buildings

### Pricing Strategy
| Product | Price | Target Customer |
|---------|-------|----------------|
| Standard mud interlocking block | ₹28/block | Residential builders |
| Premium (IGBC certified) | ₹35/block | Green buildings, institutions |
| Bulk contract (>50,000 blocks) | ₹26/block | Large housing projects |

*Market price for CSEB is ₹30–50/block (Brick&Bolt, 2026). We enter below market while still earning ~46% gross margin.*

### Distribution
- **Direct sales team** (1–2 people) calling on contractors and builders in Ranga Reddy
- **Architect/engineer relationships** — specification selling (get brick specified in project designs)
- **PMAY/govt project bids** — tender-based supply
- **WhatsApp catalog + Google Business listing** — capture the 86.8% of buyers who can't find suppliers online

## 8. Competitive Advantage (Moat)

| Advantage | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **First mover** | Zero CSEB manufacturers in Telangana. No direct competitor. |
| **Cost structure** | No kiln = 30% lower energy cost vs. fired bricks. Local soil = near-zero raw material cost. |
| **Labor savings for buyer** | Interlocking design reduces buyer's construction labor by 25–35%. Sell the total cost, not just the brick price. |
| **Digital presence** | Build website, Google Maps, WhatsApp catalog — instantly in top 13.2% of the industry. |
| **Certification** | BIS + IGBC certification = government project eligibility. Competitors can't supply to green mandated projects. |
| **Soil IP** | Test and optimize local soil mix ratios. Build a proprietary blend database for different districts. |

## 9. Go-to-Market Plan

### Month 1–3 (Pre-launch)
- Register company (MSME Udyam, GST, factory license)
- Join Telangana Builders Federation / CII real estate chapter
- Meet 20 contractors in Ranga Reddy — get letters of intent before plant is ready

### Month 4–6 (Launch)
- Begin production — supply to 3–5 pilot customers at cost price (seed the market)
- Document construction speed comparison — video content for builder community
- Apply for PMAY empanelment as eco-brick supplier

### Month 7–12 (Growth)
- Full price sales, target 10 active builder accounts
- Submit for IGBC material listing
- Hire 1 more sales executive for Hyderabad city accounts
- First 6-month supply contract with a housing developer

### Year 2
- Expand to Kamareddy plant
- Apply for Telangana Govt "Green Manufacturing" support schemes
- Explore export to Andhra Pradesh and Maharashtra
